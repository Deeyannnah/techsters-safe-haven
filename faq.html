FGM refers to all procedures involving partial or total removal of the external femal genitalia or other injury to the female genetial organs for cultural or other non- medical reasons.

The effect of FGM depends on several factors, including the type performed, the experience of the practitioner, the hygiene conditions under which it is performed, the amount of resistance and the general health condition of the girl/woman undergoing the procedure. complication may occur in all types of FGM but most frequent with infibulation. imediate complication include severe pain, shock, heamorrhage, tetanus or infection.

It may take months or a year for a full recovery after a mutilation. This includes the return of color, sensation and sexual function.

Women who have undergone the most extensive form of FGM are significantly more likely to be infertile. The risk of infertility is related to the extent of anatomical mutilation and not simply the extent of which the vulva had been sutured or closed.
