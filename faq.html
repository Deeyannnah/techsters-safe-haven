Safe Haven is an online platform or community that supports women and girls who have experienced or are suffering from female genital mutilation (FGM) and child marriage. Our solution focuses on the girl child's traumas, especially those influenced by tradition and ancestral beliefs and ways we can help her overcome these traumas.

Safe Haven works with communities to help reach children females suffering from gender-based trauma. A fundamental component of this work is realizing the inequality that consequently violates girls’ rights and working with all genders to end injustices that disproportionately harm girls like gender-based violence, limited or no educational opportunities, child marriage and early pregnancy.

Most of the 31 countries where FGM is practiced have legislated against it. However, until social norms change, the practice continues in secret. Safe Haven and other organizations are educating and empowering girls and their communities to end FGM. There’s strong evidence that it can be eliminated in one generation.

The effect of FGM depends on several factors, including the type performed, the experience of the practitioner, the hygiene conditions under which it is performed, the amount of resistance and the general health condition of the girl/woman undergoing the procedure. complication may occur in all types of FGM but most frequent with infibulation. imediate complication include severe pain, shock, heamorrhage, tetanus or infection.
